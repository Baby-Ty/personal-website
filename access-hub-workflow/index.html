Step by step

The full request lifecycle.

Every IT request follows the same path — from the moment a client hits send, to the moment the team has everything they need.

1

Client sends an email

The client emails our service desk mailbox — exactly as they normally would. No portal, no ticket system, no changes to how they work.

Fromsarah.johnson@acmecorp.co.za
Tosupport@netsurit.com
SubjectNew starter — Emma Clarke
Hi, we have a new starter joining soon — Emma Clarke in Finance. Can you get her set up please? Thanks, Sarah.
2

Received and matched to a client

The email arrives via our service desk mailbox. The sender's domain is matched against our client list and the request is automatically attributed to the right organisation.

3

Reads the email and extracts the data

The email body is sent to Claude, which reads the message, identifies the type of request, and pulls out every relevant field it can find. It also flags anything that's missing or ambiguous.

7

One click — account actioned, credentials sent

The technician clicks Approve & action. The system creates the Microsoft 365 account in Entra, assigns the license, grants shared mailbox access, generates a temporary password, and emails the credentials to the requester — all in one step.

For leavers: the same one-click approval disables the account in Entra, blocks sign-in, revokes active sessions, and applies any mailbox forwarding or delegation rules specified in the request. No steps to remember, no end-of-day rush.

Every request is logged end to end — who sent it, what was extracted, when the form was viewed and submitted, and what actions were taken.

What it handles

Types of tickets it can handle.

Claude recognises these request types automatically — no keywords, no subject line formatting, no rules to maintain.

New starter

Name, start date, department, manager, job title, license, shared mailbox access, group memberships.
✓ One-click approval

Leaver

Target user, leaving date, mailbox handling, forwarding address, delegate access.
✓ One-click approval

Shared mailbox

Target user, mailbox address, permission type (full access, send as, send on behalf).
✓ One-click approval

Group / DL change

Target user, group or distribution list name, action (add or remove).
✓ One-click approval

Access change

Target user, system or resource, action (grant, modify, revoke), details.
✓ One-click approval

MFA / password reset

Target user, reset type (MFA, password, or both), details or reason.
✓ One-click approval
The people involved

Three roles. Each with a clear part to play.

The client sends an email the way they always have. We get a complete ticket ready to action. Access Hub handles everything in between.

👤

The client

Sends a request by email as they normally would. Gets a pre-filled form link back, fills in any missing details, and submits. No portal, no account, no training required.

They never need to
✕ Log in to a portal
✕ Go back and forth with the helpdesk
✕ Follow up to chase progress
⚡

Access Hub

Reads the email, identifies the request type, extracts every field it can find, sends the form link, updates the ConnectWise ticket, and provisions or disables accounts on approval. All automated, all logged.

Happens automatically
✓ Email read & classified
✓ Form sent to requester
✓ ConnectWise ticket updated
✓ Account provisioned on approval
🛠️

The technician

Gets notified of a ticket in ConnectWise. Reviews the details, clicks approve, and it's done. No re-reading emails, no copy-pasting, no manual work.

They no longer need to
✕ Chase missing information
✕ Find and copy admin credentials
✕ Manually create accounts
✕ Email credentials to clients
